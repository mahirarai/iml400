In “Mother Cyborg is here to escort you into the future with love,” Diana Nucera aka Mother Cyborg talks about her work around media, technology, and transformative community organizing, specifically through her work in Detroit. Through Detroit Digital Justice Coalition and Equitable Internet Initiative (EII), community members and leaders used media and technology to restore community and create new economies that were rooted in local relationships, authentic communication, participation, common ownership, and digital justice. Decolonizing tech education was a significant theme in this talk and in Mother Cyborg’s work, especially in the context of transforming communities. In EII, they use a pedagogy that includes practicing popular education, starting from the end goal, using the 4mat model of learning that understands how humans learn in cycles, and focusing on facilitation skills and building relationships with community members. Through all this work, while people are learning how to build their own network and developing tech skills, the power and significance of these projects is through how the community is transformed, and how they're able to build their own capacities and have autonomy over the technologies that they engage with.
